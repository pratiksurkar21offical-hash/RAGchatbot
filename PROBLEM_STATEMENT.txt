PROBLEM STATEMENT - AI TELECOM SUPPORT ASSISTANT

For: PRD Exercise
Your task: Read this brief, then write a PRD for the product described below.


THE SITUATION

NovaCell is a mobile operator with ~4 million subscribers. Customer support is one of its biggest costs and lowest satisfaction scores. Most support contacts are Tier-1,
self-resolvable questions - issues whose answers already exist in NovaCell's own
documentation. The most common themes are:

- Slow or dropped mobile data
- Confusing or unexpected charges on a bill
- SIM / eSIM activation problems
- Roaming setup before travel
- Voice-call issues
- Basic account and app questions

Today these answers are scattered across three disconnected sources - a public FAQ, a
database of past resolved tickets, and official PDF user guides. No single surface lets a
customer ask a plain-language question and get a trustworthy answer from all three at once.
So customers wait on hold for things an FAQ could solve, agents burn out on repetitive
tickets, and answers are inconsistent.


THE PRODUCT TO SPEC

NovaCell wants an AI-powered self-serve chat assistant that answers Tier-1 customer
questions in plain language, grounded only in NovaCell's verified knowledge (FAQ, past
tickets, and guides) - never inventing prices, policies, or facts. When it can't answer
confidently, it must say so.

Build the PRD around these fixed constraints:

- AI/chat is the solution - assume a conversational assistant powered by a language model.
- Grounded answers only - every answer must trace back to NovaCell's sources; no made-up info.
- Three knowledge sources - FAQ, resolved tickets, and PDF guides, kept current by support
  ops without an engineering release.
- No live billing/CRM access in v1 - no personalized account data ("my balance"); decide how
  to handle that boundary.
- Graceful escalation - define when and how the bot hands off to a human.


YOUR PRD SHOULD COVER

1. Problem & objective
2. Target users & key use cases
3. Scope - what's in v1 and explicitly out
4. Functional requirements
5. Non-functional requirements (accuracy, latency, privacy, cost)
6. Success metrics - how you'll prove v1 worked
7. Risks, assumptions, and open questions
8. Future iterations (v2+)
