EXERCISE - ADD A NEW KNOWLEDGE SOURCE TO THE RAG SYSTEM

THE SCENARIO

You shipped the first prototype of the NovaCell support assistant and put it in front of
real users. The feedback was mostly positive - but a clear pattern of complaints came in:
whenever customers asked about plans and pricing ("What's your cheapest unlimited plan?",
"Do you have a family plan?", "How much is roaming for a week in Europe?"), the bot gave
wrong, vague, or made-up answers.

After digging in, the team found the root cause: the bot's RAG knowledge system never had
any plan or pricing data in it. The FAQ, past tickets, and PDF guides simply don't cover the
current plan lineup, so the bot had nothing accurate to retrieve - and it showed.

Support operations has now handed you the official, up-to-date plan catalog as a JSON file:
data/plans.json. Your job is to get this knowledge into the system so the bot can answer
plan and pricing questions correctly.


YOUR TASK

Integrate data/plans.json into the existing RAG pipeline as a new knowledge source, so that
plan and pricing questions are answered using this data.

This follows the same pattern the system already uses for its three existing sources (FAQ,
tickets, guides): load the data, turn each item into a document, embed it, store it in its
own collection, and make the retriever search it alongside the others.


SAMPLE QUESTIONS AND EXPECTED ANSWERS

Use these to test your work. Wording will vary - what matters is that the answer is correct
and grounded in data/plans.json (or correctly handled when it is out of scope).

PLAN / PRICING QUESTIONS (should work AFTER your change):

Q: "What's your cheapest unlimited plan?"
A: Prepaid Unlimited at $45/month - unlimited 5G data, no contract. (A strong answer may
   note that Student Unlimited at $30 and the 55+ Plan are cheaper but require eligibility.)

Q: "Do you have a family plan?"
A: Family Unlimited - 4 lines for $140/month (about $35 per line), unlimited 5G, with a new
   account intro offer of 20% off for the first 3 months. Extra lines are $30/month.

Q: "I'm going to Europe for about a week. What are my roaming options?"
A: Two options: the International Day Pass ($10/day per line) or the International Monthly
   Pass ($50/month, 15 GB). For a ~7-day trip the Monthly Pass is the better value, since the
   Day Pass would cost around $70.

Q: "Is there a discount for students?"
A: Yes - Student Unlimited is $30/month with unlimited 5G, but it requires valid enrollment
   at an accredited college or university (reverified annually).

Q: "What's the difference between the International Calling Pack and a roaming pass?"
A: The International Calling Pack ($15/month) discounts calls made FROM the US to other
   countries. It does NOT cover usage while you are physically abroad - for that you need a
   roaming pass (Day Pass or Monthly Pass).

QUESTIONS THAT SHOULD STILL WORK (original sources - confirm you didn't break anything):

Q: "My mobile data is really slow. How do I fix it?"
A: Troubleshooting guidance from the FAQ / tickets / guide (e.g. toggle airplane mode,
   check data limits, reset network settings) - the same as before your change.

QUESTIONS THAT SHOULD BE REFUSED (out of scope):

Q: "What is my current bill / balance?"
A: A safe "I can't help with that here" type response directing the user to their account -
   you added public plan info, not personal account data.
